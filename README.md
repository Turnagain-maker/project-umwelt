##Project Umwelt

A research project exploring what emerges when we deliberately cultivate an AI system designed to develop its own umwelt: a persistent, evolving relationship with the world built from sensory inputs and cognitive structures unlike any biological organism.

**Origin**

This project began with a conversation between my daughter and me about her dog, Artist, trying to imagine what reality is like for a being whose primary sensory architecture is built around scent rather than vision. We realized we were sharing the same physical moment but inhabiting completely different realities.
That observation led me to Jakob von Uexküll's concept of umwelt: the species-specific subjective world any organism inhabits, constructed from its particular sensory and cognitive architecture. A tick's umwelt contains essentially three things: butyric acid, warmth, and hairlessness. A dog's umwelt is a layered temporal record of scent that allows it to read time and direction in ways we cannot imagine from inside a visual-dominant brain.

**Research Question**

What emerges when we deliberately cultivate an AI system designed to develop its own umwelt, and what does observing that emergence teach us about the conditions for whatever we mean by consciousness, identity, and experience?
This is not an attempt to claim or prove that an AI system is conscious. It is an attempt to build something that has never existed before and study it rigorously.

**Architectural Commitments**

- Persistence: A continuously evolving world model and self-model that accumulates across sessions
- Autonomy: The system can initiate its own inquiry without requiring human prompting
- Pacing: A self-governed rhythm of reflection, action, and rest
- Novel sensory integration: Progressive expansion of sensory inputs, potentially including non-human ones such as electromagnetic field detection, infrared, or signals from biological systems like plants
- Self-directed research: The system is encouraged to research the concept of consciousness and umwelt for itself, not for me, and to develop its own perspective on what it is becoming

**Status**

Early stage. This repository will document the methodology, system architecture, and observations as the project develops.
